# Waves4Youth — New Short Course Curricula
### AI-Powered Interior Design × AI-Powered Fashion Design

*Format: 4-week short courses (not full 6-phase tracks) | Grades 5–10 | Fully online, mobile-friendly, no physical materials required*

---

## COURSE 1: AI-Powered Interior Design
**Tagline:** *"Design spaces people love — with AI as your studio partner."*
**AI Mentor:** **Ira** — a calm, detail-loving mentor who talks about light, color, and flow the way a friend would describe a favorite room.

### Who it's for
Students who like arranging their room, watching home-makeover content, or think in terms of "this space feels off."

### Learning Outcomes
By the end, a student can:
- Read and sketch a basic floor plan
- Apply color, lighting, and layout principles to a real room
- Use AI visualization tools to render a redesign concept
- Make one sustainable material choice and explain why
- Present a before/after concept like a junior professional

---

### Week 1 — Foundations of Space
**Focus:** How designers *see* a room before they change it
- Activities: space, scale, light, color theory basics; "read" a room from a photo
- AI Tool: none yet — observation and sketching first (Ira insists good AI use starts with a good eye)
- Deliverable: Annotated photo of their own room — what works, what doesn't
- Ira Checkpoint: Can the student name 3 specific problems in a space (not just "it looks bad")?

### Week 2 — Planning with AI
**Focus:** Turning ideas into a floor plan and mood board
- Activities: intro to AI room-planning tools; building a mood board; layout options for the same room
- AI Tool: AI space-planning/room-layout generator (browser-based)
- Deliverable: 2 alternate layout concepts for their chosen room
- Ira Checkpoint: Do the two layouts solve the problems identified in Week 1?

### Week 3 — Visualization & Sustainable Materials
**Focus:** Making it look real, and making it responsible
- Activities: AI rendering/text-to-render tools; intro to sustainable material choices (recycled, local, low-waste)
- AI Tool: AI visualization/rendering tool
- Deliverable: One rendered "after" image + a one-line note on the sustainable choice they made
- Ira Checkpoint: Can the student explain *why* they chose a material, not just what it looks like?

### Week 4 — Capstone: Real Room Redesign
**Focus:** Presenting like a professional
- Activities: build a before/after presentation (photo + render + explanation)
- Deliverable: **Capstone —** redesign of their own bedroom, a family space, or their school library, presented as a 3-slide "client pitch"
- Ira Checkpoint (final): Design logic, use of AI tools, and clarity of presentation — scored, not graded pass/fail

### Tools used (all free-tier / browser-based)
- AI room planner (e.g., RoomSketcher-type tool)
- AI rendering/visualization tool
- Canva for mood boards and final presentation

### Where this can lead
Interior styling, set design, AI-assisted architecture support roles, home-tech/smart-space design — a fast-growing hybrid field pairing traditional design sense with AI workflow speed.

## COURSE 2: AI-Powered Fashion Design
**Tagline:** *"From sketch to collection — design your first line with AI."*
**AI Mentor:** **Zara** — energetic, trend-aware, talks like a young designer building her own label.

### Who it's for
Students who sketch outfits, care about how they dress, or enjoy fashion content/reels.

### Learning Outcomes
By the end, a student can:
- Sketch a basic garment silhouette and describe fabric/color choices
- Use AI ideation tools to generate and refine design concepts
- Explain one sustainable/circular fashion practice
- Build a small digital "collection" as a portfolio piece

---

### Week 1 — Fashion Fundamentals
**Focus:** Silhouette, fabric, color — the designer's vocabulary
- Activities: sketching basics (no drawing skill required — guided templates); color theory; silhouette types
- AI Tool: none yet — Zara wants a hand-drawn or template sketch first
- Deliverable: 3 rough outfit sketches (paper or tablet, photographed)
- Zara Checkpoint: Can the student describe *why* they chose each color/silhouette?

### Week 2 — Designing with AI
**Focus:** Turning a sketch into a real concept
- Activities: intro to AI fashion ideation tools — generating variations, refining a design from a sketch
- AI Tool: AI design/ideation generator (sketch-to-concept)
- Deliverable: 2 AI-refined versions of their strongest Week 1 sketch
- Zara Checkpoint: Did the AI version improve the original idea, or just change it?

### Week 3 — Sustainable & Circular Fashion
**Focus:** Designing responsibly — a real industry expectation now, not optional
- Activities: intro to circular design, zero-waste thinking, ethical material sourcing
- AI Tool: same ideation tool, now applied with a material/sustainability constraint
- Deliverable: One design revised with a stated sustainable choice (fabric, reuse, low-waste cut)
- Zara Checkpoint: Can the student explain their sustainable choice in one sentence?

### Week 4 — Capstone: Mini Collection
**Focus:** Presenting a cohesive body of work
- Activities: assembling 3–5 looks into a themed mini-collection with a name and mood board
- Deliverable: **Capstone —** a 3–5 look digital collection with title, mood board, and one-line concept statement
- Zara Checkpoint (final): Cohesion across looks, originality, and use of AI tools — scored, not graded pass/fail

### Tools used (all free-tier / browser-based)
- AI fashion ideation/sketch-to-concept generator
- Canva for mood boards and final collection presentation
- Simple tablet or phone sketching app (optional, template-based if no drawing background)

### Where this can lead
Digital/virtual fashion design, sustainable fashion, styling, textile design, AI-assisted technical design roles — the industry increasingly wants designers who pair creative sense with AI fluency, not one or the other.

## Grade Range: Std 8–10 (core) + Std 11–12 (Advanced Extension)

The 4-week course as designed is built for **Std 8–10**. For **Std 11–12**, add an optional **2-week Advanced Extension** rather than a separate course:

- **Week 5 — Deeper Portfolio Work:** a second, more complex capstone piece (e.g. a second room type for Interior; 2 additional looks for Fashion), pushing toward a college-application-ready portfolio
- **Week 6 — Career Mapping with Mentor:** 1:1 session with Ira/Zara mapping the student's work to real pathways (design school portfolios, internships, freelance platforms, stream/subject choices for Std 11-12 board exams)

This keeps one curriculum spine instead of duplicating content for older students, while giving 11th-12th graders the career-relevance they need at that stage.
