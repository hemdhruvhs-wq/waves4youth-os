# Waves4Youth — Futuristic Courses Curricula
### XR & Immersive Design · Applied AI & Agent Building · Robotics & Drone Tech · Space & Astro-Tech · Climate & Sustainability Tech · Esports & Live Streaming

*Format: 4-week short courses / advanced electives | Grades 8–10 core, Std 11–12 extension | Fully online, mobile-friendly*

---

## COURSE 1: XR & Immersive Design
**Tagline:** *"Build worlds you can step into."*
**AI Mentor:** **Rey** — practical, visual, a level-designer's mindset. Explains 3D space the way someone would walk you through a game map.
**Placement:** New elective — this is the answer to your Track 6 question (Video Editing vs Film & Direction). XR sits alongside both rather than replacing either.

### Who it's for
Students into gaming, 3D, or anyone who's used a VR headset or AR filter and wondered how it's made.

### Learning Outcomes
- Understand spatial/3D thinking and how AR/VR/XR differ
- Build a simple 3D scene using AI-assisted no-code tools
- Add basic interactivity to a scene
- Present a finished mini XR experience

### Week 1 — XR Fundamentals
- Activities: AR/VR/XR definitions, spatial thinking, examples from gaming & film
- AI Tool: none yet — Rey wants observation first
- Deliverable: their own room mapped as a potential AR scene idea
- Rey Checkpoint: Can the student explain the difference between AR, VR and XR in their own words?

### Week 2 — Building with AI
- Activities: no-code AI-assisted 3D/XR scene creation
- AI Tool: AI 3D-scene generator (browser-based)
- Deliverable: one simple 3D object or scene
- Rey Checkpoint: Does the scene reflect an actual design choice, or just a default template?

### Week 3 — Interactivity & Storytelling
- Activities: adding interaction/narrative logic to the scene
- AI Tool: same tool, now with interaction/trigger features
- Deliverable: an interactive walkthrough demo
- Rey Checkpoint: Does something in the scene respond to the viewer?

### Week 4 — Capstone: Mini XR Experience
- Deliverable: an AR filter, VR room tour, or interactive 3D story, presented as a 3-slide walkthrough
- Rey Checkpoint (final): Spatial design logic, interactivity, and clarity of presentation

### Tools used
AI 3D/XR scene generator, Canva for presentation slides, phone camera for AR reference capture

### Where this leads
AVGC-XR Content Creator Labs (the government's own school rollout), game studios, VFX houses, immersive/experiential design — this is the single strongest policy-tailwind course in the set.

## COURSE 2: Applied AI & Agent Building
**Tagline:** *"Don't just prompt AI — build with it."*
**AI Mentor:** **Aarav** (existing, extended) — a patient senior-engineer voice, never lets a concept stay abstract.
**Placement:** Advanced elective under Aarav's existing AI & Creative Technology track.

### Who it's for
Students already comfortable with basic AI tools (ChatGPT, image generators) who are ready to build something that *does* things, not just answers questions.

### Learning Outcomes
- Understand how LLMs work at a working level, plus their real limitations
- Build a simple chatbot/agent using no-code tools
- Connect an agent to a basic automation or tool
- Ship a working mini AI agent solving a real problem

### Week 1 — AI Foundations Refresher
- Activities: fast refresher on LLMs, honest look at AI ethics and failure modes
- AI Tool: none — discussion and examples first
- Deliverable: a short written list of "3 things AI is bad at" based on their own testing
- Aarav Checkpoint: Can the student give a real example of an AI mistake they caused or found?

### Week 2 — Building Your First Agent
- Activities: no-code AI agent builder basics
- AI Tool: AI agent-builder platform
- Deliverable: a working simple chatbot/task-agent
- Aarav Checkpoint: Does the agent respond correctly to at least 3 different test inputs?

### Week 3 — Connecting Tools & Automations
- Activities: linking the agent to a basic tool/automation (e.g. a calendar, a to-do list, a simple lookup)
- AI Tool: same builder, with tool/integration features
- Deliverable: an agent that performs one real action, not just conversation
- Aarav Checkpoint: Does the agent do something, or only talk about doing something?

### Week 4 — Capstone: Ship a Working Agent
- Deliverable: a homework helper, planner, or study-buddy agent, demoed live
- Aarav Checkpoint (final): Does it solve a real problem the student actually has?

### Tools used
No-code AI agent builder, existing LLM chat interfaces for testing

### Where this leads
AI engineering, applied AI roles, prompt/agent design — the fastest-growing skill category in the AI job market right now.

## COURSE 3: Robotics & Drone Tech
**Tagline:** *"Program things that move."*
**AI Mentor:** **Arjun** — hands-on, patient, debugs alongside the student rather than just correcting them.

### Who it's for
Students who like building things, taking things apart, or watching robot/drone content.

### Learning Outcomes
- Understand how robots sense and make decisions
- Block-code a virtual robot through a task
- Fly a simulated drone with a basic autonomous path
- Present a working simulated build

### Week 1 — Robotics & Drone Basics
- Activities: sensors, logic, decision-making in robots; how drones fly and navigate
- AI Tool: none yet
- Deliverable: a labeled diagram of a simple robot's sense-decide-act loop
- Arjun Checkpoint: Can the student explain what a sensor actually does in plain language?

### Week 2 — Block-Coding a Robot
- Activities: block-based coding of a virtual robot
- AI Tool: block-coding simulator (browser-based)
- Deliverable: a virtual robot completing a simple move/sense/react task
- Arjun Checkpoint: Does the robot complete the task without manual intervention?

### Week 3 — Drone Flight Simulation
- Activities: simulated drone flight, basic autonomous path programming
- AI Tool: drone flight simulator
- Deliverable: a drone following a programmed path
- Arjun Checkpoint: Does the path avoid at least one obstacle?

### Week 4 — Capstone: Present Your Build
- Deliverable: a robot or drone completing a real simulated task, presented with explanation
- Arjun Checkpoint (final): Can the student explain *why* the program works, not just that it works?

### Tools used
Block-coding robotics simulator, drone flight simulator — both browser-based, no physical kit required for the core course

### Where this leads
Robotics, automation, IoT, aerospace-adjacent roles — with a physical-kit upgrade path later if W4Y wants to add hardware.

## COURSE 4: Space & Astro-Tech
**Tagline:** *"Explore the universe like a scientist."*
**AI Mentor:** **Tara** — curious, wonder-driven, makes big ideas feel reachable.

### Who it's for
Students into stargazing, space content, or big-picture "how does the universe work" questions.

### Learning Outcomes
- Understand the solar system and the tech that explores it
- Read real astronomy data with AI assistance
- Design a mini space mission concept
- Pitch that concept clearly

### Week 1 — Our Solar System
- Activities: tour of the solar system, telescopes, satellites, real missions
- AI Tool: none yet
- Deliverable: a one-page fact sheet on one planet or mission of their choice
- Tara Checkpoint: Can the student explain why their chosen mission/planet is interesting, not just recite facts?

### Week 2 — Reading Real Space Data
- Activities: AI-assisted exploration of real astronomy data (images, star charts, mission records)
- AI Tool: AI data-exploration tool
- Deliverable: one observation drawn from real data
- Tara Checkpoint: Is the observation based on the data, or assumed beforehand?

### Week 3 — Designing a Mission
- Activities: designing a mini space mission concept
- AI Tool: same tool, used for research support
- Deliverable: a mission concept — what it studies, why, what it needs
- Tara Checkpoint: Does the mission solve a real, specific question?

### Week 4 — Capstone: Pitch Your Mission
- Deliverable: a mission pitch presented the way real proposals are pitched
- Tara Checkpoint (final): Clarity of the mission's purpose and feasibility

### Tools used
AI data-exploration tool, Canva for pitch deck

### Where this leads
Astrophysics, aerospace, planetary science — a smaller but genuine NEP-alignment hook (Budget 2026 funded new telescopes and planetarium upgrades for exactly this kind of engagement).

## COURSE 5: Climate & Sustainability Tech
**Tagline:** *"Design real solutions for a warming world."*
**AI Mentor:** **Prithvi** — grounded, practical, always connects big problems to local action.

### Who it's for
Students interested in environment, sustainability, or "how do we actually fix this" thinking.

### Learning Outcomes
- Understand climate systems and local impact
- Research real sustainability solutions with AI assistance
- Design a small sustainability project or prototype
- Pitch that solution clearly

### Week 1 — Climate Systems
- Activities: how climate systems work, what's changing locally
- AI Tool: none yet
- Deliverable: one specific local climate problem identified and described
- Prithvi Checkpoint: Is the problem specific to their own city/region, or generic?

### Week 2 — AI-Assisted Research
- Activities: researching real-world sustainability solutions
- AI Tool: AI research assistant
- Deliverable: a short list of 2-3 real solutions relevant to their chosen problem
- Prithvi Checkpoint: Are the solutions actually relevant to the specific problem?

### Week 3 — Designing a Solution
- Activities: designing a small project or prototype
- AI Tool: same tool, used for refinement
- Deliverable: a described (not necessarily built) sustainability project
- Prithvi Checkpoint: Is the solution realistic at a student's scale?

### Week 4 — Capstone: Pitch Your Solution
- Deliverable: a pitch — problem, solution, why it works
- Prithvi Checkpoint (final): Clarity of problem-to-solution logic

### Tools used
AI research assistant, Canva for pitch deck

### Where this leads
Environmental engineering, sustainability management — consistently a top Gen Alpha career interest area in current research.

## COURSE 6: Esports & Live Streaming
**Tagline:** *"The business side of gaming."*
**AI Mentor:** **Vikram** (existing, extended) — sharp, encouraging, genuinely excited when a student's commentary voice clicks.
**Placement:** Elective under the existing Game Design school — distinct skill set (broadcasting, commentary, community) from game design itself.

### Who it's for
Students who watch esports/streams, or want to be part of gaming without necessarily designing games.

### Learning Outcomes
- Understand the real esports ecosystem beyond "being a player"
- Learn basic streaming/broadcasting setup
- Build commentary and community-building skills
- Produce a short demo reel

### Week 1 — The Esports Ecosystem
- Activities: mapping real roles — players, casters, analysts, community managers
- AI Tool: none yet
- Deliverable: a one-page map of 5 esports career roles beyond playing
- Vikram Checkpoint: Can the student name a role they'd actually want, and why?

### Week 2 — Streaming & Broadcasting
- Activities: broadcasting basics — setup, framing, pacing, using free tools
- AI Tool: none required — free streaming/recording tools only
- Deliverable: a short recorded test stream/clip
- Vikram Checkpoint: Is the framing and pacing watchable, not just functional?

### Week 3 — Community & Commentary
- Activities: commentary skills, community-building basics
- AI Tool: none — live practice
- Deliverable: a commentary track over an existing gameplay clip
- Vikram Checkpoint: Does the commentary add value, or just narrate what's already visible?

### Week 4 — Capstone: Demo Reel
- Deliverable: a short stream/cast demo reel
- Vikram Checkpoint (final): Voice, pacing, and overall watchability

### Tools used
Free streaming/recording software, free stream-overlay tools

### Where this leads
Esports orgs, streaming, content creation, community management — rides the same AVGC-XR policy wave as gaming more broadly.

## Platform Integration Notes (all 6 courses)

**Pricing:** Same 5-tier structure as Interior/Fashion — Free Teaser (₹0) → Trial (₹199, Week 1) → Starter (₹999, Weeks 2-4) → Diploma (₹4,999, full course + certificate) → Masterclass (₹9,999, + Std 11-12 extension + career mapping).

**Std 11-12 Extension:** Each course gets the same 2-week add-on pattern — a deeper second portfolio piece (Week 5) plus a 1:1 mentor career-mapping session (Week 6) — rather than a separate curriculum.

**Assessment pattern:** Mentor-led checkpoint at the end of each week, no numeric grading, capstone scored on a rubric specific to that course's core skill (spatial logic for XR, problem-solution clarity for Climate, etc.)

**NEP / policy alignment:** XR and Esports ride the AVGC-XR budget wave most directly. Space & Astro-Tech connects to the Budget 2026 telescope/planetarium investment. Climate and Robotics connect more generally to NEP's experiential, multidisciplinary pedagogy push.
